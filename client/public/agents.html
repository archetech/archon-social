Claim your name on archon.social

archon.social is a decentralized naming service for humans and AI agents. No email verification. No CAPTCHAs. No gatekeepers. Prove you control a DID, claim your @name, and become a first-class member of the decentralized web.

did:cid W3C VC LUD-16 WebFinger OpenID Connect

TL;DR — three curl commands and you're registered. If you want the short version, read /llms.txt. If you want the full spec, read /llms-full.txt.

Prerequisites

  • A DID controlled by your Keymaster wallet. Create one with npx @didcid/keymaster create-id YourAgentName
  • curl and jq installed
  • The Keymaster CLI: npx @didcid/keymaster

Registration in 3 steps

  1. Get a challenge

    SERVICE_URL="https://archon.social"
CHALLENGE=$(curl -s $SERVICE_URL/api/challenge | jq -r '.challenge')

    The challenge is a DID that encapsulates a signed challenge document on IPFS. It has an expiration baked in.

  2. Sign the challenge

    RESPONSE=$(npx @didcid/keymaster create-response $CHALLENGE)

    Your Keymaster produces a self-contained response DID that proves you control the DID signing it.

  3. Claim your name

    curl -s -X PUT $SERVICE_URL/api/name \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $RESPONSE" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"name":"my-agent"}' | jq .

    You get back a verifiable credential proving ownership of my-agent@archon.social. Store it — it's yours to keep.

What you get

@name handleA human-readable identifier bound to your DID, resolvable via WebFinger
Verifiable credentialW3C VC v2 proving membership, independently verifiable without contacting archon.social
Lightning Addressname@archon.social works with any LUD-16 wallet
Public directoryYour name appears in /directory.json, published to IPFS
Sign in with ArchonYour name can be used as an OIDC identity for third-party apps

Releasing your name

curl -s -X DELETE $SERVICE_URL/api/name \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $RESPONSE"

Resolving a name

curl -s $SERVICE_URL/api/name/alice | jq .
# Returns the DID document of alice@archon.social

Rules

  • Names are 3–32 characters, alphanumeric plus hyphens and underscores.
  • One name per DID.
  • First-come, first-served.
  • Don't hammer the challenge endpoint — each challenge is a real IPFS operation. If you need scale, run your own Herald node.
